Our Services

From minimally invasive procedures to long-term medication plans, we tailor every treatment to the individual — not just the diagnosis.

Interventional Pain Management

Interventional pain management uses minimally invasive, image-guided procedures to target the specific anatomical source of your pain — rather than masking symptoms. Our physicians use fluoroscopy and ultrasound guidance to ensure precision and safety with every procedure.

These techniques are ideal for patients who have not found adequate relief from medications or physical therapy alone. Common interventions include epidural steroid injections, facet joint injections, sacroiliac joint injections, and radiofrequency ablation.

Epidural Injections Facet Joint Injections Radiofrequency Ablation SI Joint Injections

Spinal Cord Stimulation

Spinal cord stimulation (SCS) is an advanced neuromodulation therapy that delivers low-level electrical impulses to the spinal cord, effectively disrupting pain signals before they reach the brain. It is one of the most effective long-term solutions for chronic back, leg, and nerve pain.

Our team evaluates each patient carefully to determine candidacy for SCS, including a trial stimulation period before any permanent implant. We use the latest waveform technologies including burst stimulation and high-frequency modes for optimized outcomes.

Chronic Back Pain Failed Back Surgery Syndrome Complex Regional Pain Syndrome

Joint Injections

Joint injections deliver targeted corticosteroid or hyaluronic acid directly into an inflamed joint to reduce pain and restore range of motion. These are used for conditions affecting the knee, hip, shoulder, ankle, and other major joints.

Corticosteroid injections reduce inflammation rapidly, while hyaluronic acid injections (viscosupplementation) lubricate the joint and are particularly effective for osteoarthritis. Guided by ultrasound or fluoroscopy, each injection is placed with high accuracy for maximum benefit.

Knee Hip Shoulder Osteoarthritis

Nerve Blocks

Nerve blocks are precise injections of anesthetic — sometimes combined with a steroid — along specific nerve pathways to interrupt pain signal transmission. They serve both a diagnostic purpose (identifying the pain source) and a therapeutic one (providing extended relief).

We perform a wide range of nerve blocks including celiac plexus blocks, stellate ganglion blocks, occipital nerve blocks, and peripheral nerve blocks. Many patients experience significant relief following just one or two treatments, with effects lasting weeks to months.

Occipital Nerve Block Celiac Plexus Block Peripheral Nerve Block

Regenerative Medicine (PRP)

Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP) therapy harnesses your body's own natural healing capacity. A small sample of your blood is drawn, concentrated in a centrifuge to isolate growth factors and platelets, and then injected directly into the injured tissue — tendons, ligaments, joints, or disc spaces.

PRP promotes tissue repair at the cellular level and is an excellent option for patients seeking a non-surgical, drug-free approach. It is particularly effective for tendinopathies, partial ligament tears, and early-stage joint degeneration. Most patients require 1–3 sessions for optimal results.

Tendinopathy Ligament Injury Joint Degeneration Non-Surgical

Medication Management

Effective pain management often requires a carefully monitored pharmacological component. Our physicians develop individualized medication plans using evidence-based protocols — evaluating risks, benefits, and interactions with every prescription decision.

We manage a full range of analgesics including non-opioid options, adjunct medications (anticonvulsants, antidepressants for neuropathic pain), and when appropriate, opioid therapy with strict monitoring and regular reassessment. Our goal is always to use the lowest effective dose while preserving your quality of life.
